  1. Perpetual Peace
    1. Introduction
    2. First section: Containing the preliminary articles for perpetual peace among states
      1. Article 1: No treaty of peace shall be regarded as valid, if made with the secret reservation of material for a future war
      2. Article 2: No state having an independent existence—whether it be great or small—shall be acquired by another through inheritance, exchange, purchase or donation
      3. Article 3: Standing armies (miles perpetuus) shall be abolished in course of time
      4. Article 4: No national debts shall be contracted in connection with the external affairs of the state
      5. Article 5: No state shall violently interfere with the constitution and administration of another
      6. Article 6: No state at war with another shall countenance such modes of hostility as would make mutual confidence impossible in a subsequent state of peace: such are the employment of assassins (percussores) or of poisoners (venefici), breaches of capitulation, the instigating and making use of treachery (perduellio) in the hostile state
      7. Conclusion
    3. Second section: Containing the definitive articles for perpetual peace among states
      1. Introduction
      2. Article Ⅰ: The civil constitution of each state shall be republican
      3. Article ⅠⅠ: The law of nations shall be founded on a federation of free states
      4. Article ⅠⅠⅠ: The rights of men, as citizens of the world, shall be limited to the conditions of universal hospitality
